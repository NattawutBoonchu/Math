Chapter 1

Number & Integers

Understanding whole numbers, negative numbers, and the four operations.

1.1 Place Value & Large Numbers

Numbers are built using digits 0–9 in place value positions. Each position is 10× the one to its right.

... Millions | Hundred-thousands | Ten-thousands | Thousands | Hundreds | Tens | Units
🔑 Key Concept

The value of a digit depends on its position. In 4,572, the 4 has a value of 4,000.

✏️ Example

Write 3,045,780 in words.

→ Three million, forty-five thousand, seven hundred and eighty.

1.2 Positive & Negative Integers

Integers include all whole numbers and their negatives: …−3, −2, −1, 0, 1, 2, 3…

🔑 Key Concept

On a number line, numbers increase going right and decrease going left. −3 < −1 < 0 < 2

✏️ Example

Calculate: −4 + 7

→ Start at −4, move 7 right = 3

Calculate: 3 − 8

→ Start at 3, move 8 left = −5

💡 Teacher Tip

Use a thermometer or vertical number line to make negatives visual and concrete.

1.3 Order of Operations (BODMAS)

When a calculation has mixed operations, use BODMAS: Brackets → Orders → Division & Multiplication (left to right) → Addition & Subtraction (left to right).

✏️ Example

Calculate: 3 + 4 × (8 − 2)

Step 1 (Brackets): 8 − 2 = 6

Step 2 (Multiply): 4 × 6 = 24

Step 3 (Add): 3 + 24 = 27

📝 Practice Questions
  1. Write 5,206,030 in words.
  2. Order from least to greatest: −7, 3, −2, 0, 5
  3. Calculate: −6 + 10 − 3
  4. Using BODMAS: (5 + 3) × 2 − 4 ÷ 2
  5. The temperature is −3°C. It drops 5°C. What is the new temperature?

📚 Key Vocabulary

TermMeaning
IntegerA whole number (positive, negative, or zero)
Place valueThe value of a digit based on its position
BODMASThe correct order to perform operations
Negative numberA number less than zero

✅ I Can …

  • Read and write large numbers using place value
  • Compare and order positive and negative integers
  • Add and subtract integers
  • Apply BODMAS to multi-step calculations
Chapter 2

Fractions, Decimals & Percentages

Connecting three ways to represent parts of a whole.

2.1 Fractions

A fraction shows part of a whole. The numerator (top) = parts we have; the denominator (bottom) = total equal parts.

🔑 Key Concept

To add/subtract fractions, find a common denominator first.
1/3 + 1/4 = 4/12 + 3/12 = 7/12

✏️ Example — Multiply fractions

2/3 × 3/5

→ Numerators: 2 × 3 = 6  |  Denominators: 3 × 5 = 15

→ Simplify: 6/15 = 2/5

2.2 Decimals

Decimals are fractions with denominators of 10, 100, 1000, etc.

0.375 = 375/1000 = 3/8
💡 Teacher Tip

Use base-10 blocks or decimal grids so students can visualise tenths and hundredths before abstract working.

2.3 Percentages

Percent means "out of 100". To convert: Fraction → Decimal → Percentage.

3/4 → 0.75 → 75%
✏️ Example

Find 30% of $250.

→ 30% = 0.30  |  0.30 × 250 = $75

📝 Practice Questions
  1. Simplify: 18/24
  2. Calculate: 3/4 + 2/3
  3. Convert 0.45 to a fraction and a percentage.
  4. Find 15% of $80.
  5. A shirt costs $60 and is 20% off. What do you pay?

✅ I Can …

  • Add, subtract, multiply, and divide fractions
  • Convert between fractions, decimals, and percentages
  • Calculate percentages of amounts
  • Apply percentages to real-world problems
Chapter 3

Ratios & Rates

Comparing quantities and understanding how things change together.

3.1 Ratios

A ratio compares two or more quantities of the same kind, written as a : b.

🔑 Key Concept

Always simplify by dividing by the HCF. 12 : 8 = 3 : 2 (÷4)

✏️ Example — Divide in a ratio

Share $60 in the ratio 2 : 3.

→ Total parts = 5  |  Each part = $60 ÷ 5 = $12

→ Share 1 = 2 × $12 = $24  |  Share 2 = 3 × $12 = $36

3.2 Rates

A rate compares two quantities of different kinds (e.g. km/h, $/kg).

✏️ Example

A car travels 240 km in 3 hours. Average speed?

→ Speed = 240 ÷ 3 = 80 km/h

📝 Practice Questions
  1. Simplify: 30 : 45
  2. Share 80 apples in ratio 3 : 5.
  3. A recipe uses flour and sugar 4 : 1. If you use 2 cups sugar, how much flour?
  4. A cyclist rides 36 km in 90 min. Find speed in km/h.
  5. Better deal: 500 g cheese for $4.50 or 750 g for $6.30?

✅ I Can …

  • Write and simplify ratios
  • Divide a quantity in a given ratio
  • Calculate rates such as speed and unit price
  • Compare rates to find the best value
Chapter 4

Algebra & Expressions

Using letters to represent unknown values and describe patterns.

4.1 Variables & Expressions

WordsExpression
A number plus 5x + 5
Three times a number3n
A number divided by 4x/4
Twice a number minus 12n − 1

4.2 Simplifying — Collecting Like Terms

✏️ Example

Simplify: 3x + 5 + 2x − 1

→ (3x + 2x) + (5 − 1) = 5x + 4

4.3 Solving Equations

🔑 Key Concept

Whatever you do to one side, do the same to the other side.

✏️ Example

Solve: 2x + 3 = 11

Step 1: − 3 both sides → 2x = 8

Step 2: ÷ 2 both sides → x = 4

Check: 2(4) + 3 = 11 ✓

4.4 Number Patterns

✏️ Example

Pattern: 5, 8, 11, 14, … (adds 3 each time)

→ Rule: T(n) = 3n + 2

→ 10th term: 3(10) + 2 = 32

📝 Practice Questions
  1. Write an expression for "6 more than twice a number".
  2. Simplify: 4a + 3b − a + 2b
  3. Solve: 3x − 5 = 16
  4. Solve: x/4 + 2 = 7
  5. Find the rule for 3, 7, 11, 15, … and find the 20th term.

✅ I Can …

  • Write algebraic expressions from word problems
  • Simplify by collecting like terms
  • Solve one- and two-step equations
  • Find and use rules for number patterns
Chapter 5

Geometry & Angles

Properties of shapes, angles, and geometric reasoning.

5.1 Types of Angles

TypeSize
AcuteLess than 90°
RightExactly 90°
ObtuseBetween 90° and 180°
StraightExactly 180°
ReflexBetween 180° and 360°

5.2 Angle Relationships

🔑 Key Concepts

Complementary angles add to 90°  |  Supplementary angles add to 180°
Vertically opposite angles are equal  |  Angles in a triangle add to 180°
Angles in a quadrilateral add to 360°

✏️ Example

A triangle has angles 65° and 48°. Find the third.

→ 180° − 65° − 48° = 67°

5.3 Properties of 2D Shapes

  • Triangle: 3 sides, angles sum to 180°
  • Quadrilateral: 4 sides, angles sum to 360°
  • Regular polygon: All sides and angles equal
  • Interior angle sum of a polygon: (n − 2) × 180°, where n = number of sides
📝 Practice Questions
  1. Classify an angle of 135°.
  2. Two supplementary angles; one is 73°. Find the other.
  3. A triangle has angles 2x°, 3x°, and 55°. Find x.
  4. Find the interior angle sum of a hexagon (6 sides).
  5. Vertically opposite angles: (3x + 10)° and (5x − 6)°. Find x.

✅ I Can …

  • Identify and classify angle types
  • Use angle relationships to find missing angles
  • Apply angle sums in triangles and quadrilaterals
  • Find interior angle sums for any polygon
Chapter 6

Area, Perimeter & Volume

Measuring 2D and 3D shapes with formulas.

6.1 Perimeter

Perimeter = total distance around the outside of a shape.

Rectangle: P = 2(l + w)  |  Triangle: P = a + b + c

6.2 Area

ShapeFormula
RectangleA = l × w
TriangleA = ½ × b × h
ParallelogramA = b × h
CircleA = π × r²
✏️ Example

Triangle with base 10 cm and height 6 cm.

→ A = ½ × 10 × 6 = 30 cm²

6.3 Volume

Rectangular prism: V = l × w × h (units: cm³ or m³)
✏️ Example

Box: 5 cm × 4 cm × 3 cm

→ V = 5 × 4 × 3 = 60 cm³

📝 Practice Questions
  1. Find the perimeter of a rectangle 8 m × 5 m.
  2. Triangle: base 12 cm, height 9 cm. Find the area.
  3. Find the area of a circle with radius 7 cm. (π ≈ 3.14)
  4. A pool is 25 m × 10 m × 2 m deep. Find its volume.
  5. A room is 6 m × 4 m. Carpet costs $35/m². What is the total cost?

✅ I Can …

  • Calculate perimeter of any polygon
  • Find the area of common 2D shapes
  • Calculate volume of rectangular prisms
  • Solve real-world measurement problems
Chapter 7

Statistics & Probability

Collecting, displaying, and interpreting data; understanding chance.

7.1 Types of Data

Data can be categorical (e.g. favourite colour) or numerical (e.g. height). Organise data in a frequency table first.

7.2 Measures of Centre & Spread

MeasureHow to Find
MeanSum of values ÷ number of values
MedianMiddle value when data is ordered
ModeMost frequently occurring value
RangeMaximum − Minimum
✏️ Example

Data: 4, 7, 2, 9, 7, 5, 3  →  Ordered: 2, 3, 4, 5, 7, 7, 9

Mean = 37 ÷ 7 ≈ 5.3  |  Median = 5  |  Mode = 7  |  Range = 7

7.3 Displaying Data

  • Bar/Column graph: Compare categories
  • Line graph: Show change over time
  • Pie chart: Show proportions of a whole
  • Stem-and-leaf plot: Show distribution of numerical data
💡 Teacher Tip

Always label axes and title your graph. Ask students "what does this graph tell you?" to build interpretation skills.

7.4 Probability

Probability measures likelihood, from 0 (impossible) to 1 (certain).

P(event) = Number of favourable outcomes ÷ Total possible outcomes
✏️ Example

A bag has 3 red, 2 blue, 5 green balls. What is P(red)?

→ P(red) = 3/10 = 0.3 = 30%

📝 Practice Questions
  1. Find mean, median, mode, range for: 8, 3, 6, 8, 5, 10, 2
  2. A standard die is rolled. Find P(even number).
  3. Favourite sports: Football 12, Swimming 8, Tennis 5, Basketball 10. Draw a bar graph.
  4. A card is drawn from a 52-card deck. What is P(Ace)?
  5. Temperatures for a week: 21, 24, 19, 23, 26, 28, 22. Find the mean.

✅ I Can …

  • Calculate mean, median, mode, and range
  • Choose and draw appropriate data displays
  • Interpret graphs and statistical measures
  • Calculate theoretical probability
